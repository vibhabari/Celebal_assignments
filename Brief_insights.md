# Brief Insights – SQL Sales Data Analysis 

The assignment is focused on analyzing sales data using SQL queries to extract meaningful business insights. Various SQL operations such as filtering, aggregation, grouping, sorting, and validation were used to study customer orders, sales performance, profitability, and regional trends.

# Key Insights

1. Dataset Exploration

The dataset contains detailed information about customer orders, product categories, sales, profits, discounts, shipping details, and regional performance. Initial exploration confirmed that the dataset was successfully imported into the SQL database.

 2. Regional Analysis

Sales and profits varied across different regions. Some regions generated significantly higher revenue and profit, indicating stronger market demand and customer activity in those areas.

 3. Category Performance

Technology products contributed the highest sales among all categories, while some categories showed lower profitability despite large sales volumes. This highlights the importance of analyzing both revenue and profit together.

 4. Sales Filtering

Filtering high-value sales transactions helped identify premium orders and customers contributing significantly to total revenue.

 5. Date-Based Analysis

Date filtering and monthly trend analysis revealed seasonal sales patterns and business growth over time. Certain months showed higher sales activity, indicating peak demand periods.

 6. Aggregation Analysis

GROUP BY queries helped summarize data efficiently by category, region, and sub-category. Aggregation functions such as SUM(), AVG(), and COUNT() were useful in identifying total sales, average sales, and product demand.

 7. Product Demand

Some sub-categories and products were sold in higher quantities than others, showing customer preference trends and popular product segments.

 8. Customer Analysis

A small group of customers contributed a major share of total sales revenue. Identifying these top customers can help businesses improve customer retention strategies.

9. Profitability Analysis

Profit analysis revealed that some products generated losses despite having good sales. High discounts and operational costs may have reduced profitability for these products.

 10. Duplicate and Data Quality Checks

Duplicate record checks and null value validation improved data reliability and ensured accurate analysis results.

Conclusion

The SQL-based sales analysis successfully demonstrated how SQL can be used for business intelligence and decision-making. By applying filtering, aggregation, sorting, and validation techniques, valuable insights were obtained regarding sales trends, customer behavior, regional performance, product profitability, and data quality.
